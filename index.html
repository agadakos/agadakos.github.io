About

I am a computer scientist whose work sits at the intersection of system security, privacy, and machine learning. My research examines how emerging AI paradigms — particularly agentic systems — introduce new attack surfaces, and how principled software engineering can harden programs against exploitation.

Before joining academia, I spent years in industry building production systems at scale: from privacy-engineering tooling at AWS to IoT security research under DARPA and DHS contracts at SRI International. This blend of rigorous research and real-world engineering shapes how I approach both scholarship and teaching.
